Until recently, touch-based solutions have remained a standard way of interacting with physical experiences. However, the health crisis is making the world completely rethink the way we interact in public and retail spaces.

From an engineering perspective, transitioning current screen-based experiences to support touchless interaction is one possible near-term solution. Touchless offers the potential for not only safer experiences but also innovating ways to interact.

Touchless experiences can be deployed for a variety of different use cases, each with their own advantages and challenges. In retail space for example, high foot traffic requires touchless solutions that can distinguish between a user's hands and those of a bystander. While in a museum setting, touchless experiences will have to be robust and adaptable enough to work in dark or unusual lighting conditions.

V_ is exploring various options for low-touch and touchless physical experiences. This site holds some of these experiments.
